UNCOVER DENMARK'S HIDDEN TREASURES IN ITS MUSEUM

Step inside Denmark’s museums and uncover stories of art, history, and culture waiting to inspire you.





Nationalmuseet

Ny Vestergade 101471 København K

The National Museum of Denmark (Nationalmuseet) in Copenhagen is the country's largest museum of cultural history. Located in the 18th-century Prince's Palace, it houses vast collections spanning 14,000 years, from the Stone Age and Viking Era to modern Danish history and foreign cultures.

Louisiana Museum of Modern Art (Humlebæk)

Gl Strandvej 13, 3050 Humlebæk, Denmark

The museum exudes an informal and relaxed atmosphere in the lovely old park and exhibition buildings, and Louisiana has something to offer everyone: the Louisiana Collection, temporary exhibitions of modern and contemporary art, photography, design and architecture exhibitions, films and concerts.

<- READ MORE





ARoS Aarhus Art Museum

Aros Allé 2, 8000 Aarhus, Denmark

ARoS is an international art museum in the centre of Aarhus, second city in Denmark. With five galleries and an entire floor dedicated to installation art, ARoS offers an important free space where it is possible to understand the past, explore the present and imagine the future in completely new ways.

Designmuseum Danmark

ABredgade 68, 1260 København, Denmark

The museum also houses Copenhagen’s greenest museum garden, a superb café, and a design shop with the classics as well as new and unique design finds. Earlier, the museum was the first public hospital in Denmark, The Royal Frederik's Hospital, where the Danish philosopher Søren Kierkegaard was admitted and died in 1855.

Viking Ship Museum in Denmark

Vindeboder 12, 4000 Roskilde, Denmark

1000 years ago, the Vikings sailed along the shores of Europe, up rivers and across the open sea throughout the North Atlantic area. Ships are the essence of the Viking Age. With them, the Scandinavian countries were founded. The Viking Ship Museum is built around the five original Viking ships from Skuldelev. They are part of a global story about ships, people and things on voyages. The museum tells the story of how the Vikings changed the World with their ships. Without ships, no Viking Age..
