GREEN JOURNEYS: DISCOVER ECO-TOURISM HERE IN DENMARK

Discover breathtaking destinations while caring for the planet. Eco-tourism lets you explore nature responsibly and create meaningful travel experiences.





CopenHill

Vindmøllevej 6, 2300 København, Denmark

The landmark CopenHill waste-to-energy plant first imagined by Bjarke Ingels Group has finally opened in Copenhagen. ArchDaily initially covered BIG's project in January 2011, and the waste-to-energy plant would later include hedonistic ideas of the world’s first steam ring generator and crowdfunding through Kickstarter. Today, the project has fulfilled many of its promises, and CopenHill stands as a modern architectural zeitgeist reflecting BIG's own evolution.

Crowne Plaza Copenhagen Towers by IHG

Copenhagen Towers, Ørestads Blvd. 114, 118, 2300 København, Denmark

Crowne Plaza Copenhagen Towers is sustainable because it uses solar panels and renewable energy to reduce carbon emissions. It has a groundwater-based heating and cooling system that saves large amounts of energy. The hotel also follows eco-friendly practices like waste recycling and serving sustainably sourced food. Its green building design and environmental certifications show its strong commitment to protecting the environment.

GreenKyak

Gormsgade 23, 7300 Jelling, Denmark

GreenKayak in Denmark is an eco-tourism initiative where people can kayak for free in exchange for collecting trash from the water. Participants help clean canals and harbors while enjoying a unique outdoor experience. The project promotes environmental awareness and sustainable travel in cities like Copenhagen. It turns simple kayaking into a meaningful activity that protects nature and supports cleaner waterways.

Oyster Safaris in Denmark

LJuvrevej 6, 6792 Rømø, Denmark

Oyster Safari in Denmark is a unique coastal adventure where visitors wade through shallow waters to harvest fresh wild oysters. It takes place along the shores of the Wadden Sea, a UNESCO-protected natural area rich in marine life. Guided tours teach participants about local ecosystems, sustainability, and traditional food culture. It’s an unforgettable eco-tourism experience that combines nature, learning, and delicious rewards.
