DISCOVER THE TIMELESS CULTURE OF DENMARK

Walk through time in Denmark, where majestic castles and timeless landmarks reveal the stories of kings, culture and history.






Waterfront Cultural Center

Amusement Park in Copenhagen, Denmark

Opening in 2025 on Copenhagen’s Paper Island, the Vandkulturhus (Water Culture House) will invite guests to swim with views of Copenhagen’s harbors through glass-paned walls. A combination of indoor and outdoor pools will create a relaxing setting that celebrates Denmark’s affinity for water. Situated in a popular urban renewal area, the Vandkulturhus will offer locals and tourists a unique place to unwind and enjoy Copenhagen’s iconic waterfront.

The Little Mermaid Statue

Langelinie in Copenhagen, Denmark

The Little Mermaid statue is located at Langelinie in Copenhagen, Denmark. Inspired by Hans Christian Andersen’s famous fairy tale, this iconic bronze statue has become one of Denmark’s most visited landmarks. Sitting gracefully by the harbor, it attracts tourists from around the world who come to admire its charm and capture a piece of Danish literary and cultural history.

Hygge

Christiansborg Palace, located in Copenhagen, Denmark, is a historic and political landmark that houses the Danish Parliament, the Prime Minister’s Office, and the Supreme Court. Built on the site of former castles dating back to the 12th century, the palace showcases a mix of architectural styles and rich royal history. Visitors can explore its grand halls, royal reception rooms, and the ruins beneath, making it both a cultural and governmental icon of Denmark.

Jante Law

Janteloven’s social code dictates emphasis on collective accomplishments and well-being, and disdains focus on individual achievements. It is an underlying Scandinavian philosophy principle that applies across Denmark, Norway, Sweden, Finland, and Iceland. Understanding Janteloven is paramount to understanding both the history and modern-day cultures of these countries.
